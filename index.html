Aphex Twin

Summary

Richard D. James, professionally known as Aphex Twin, was born on August 18, 1971 in Limerick, Ireland and raised in Cornwall, UK. He is a British electronic musician, DJ, and an influential figure of the IDM (intelligent dance music) genre.

Fun Facts

  1. He's 54 years old.
  2. His main styles of music are ambient, techno, DnB, and jungle.
  3. He founded his own record label called Rephlex Records in 1991.
  4. He's been experimenting with technology and sound all the way back to his early childhood. At the age of 12, he began composing his own music and DJing at local clubs.
  5. His first track was created on a small home computer called the Sinclair ZX81. While it had no sound hardware, James found out he could create noise by playing around with TV signals in its code.
  6. Aphex Twin isn't the only moniker he goes by. There's AFX, Caustic Window, Analogue Bubblebath, Blue Calx, and many, many more.
  7. He was supposedly named after his twin brother who died before he was born.
  8. According to his engineering lecturer in Cornwall College, he often wore headphones during lessons.
  9. A recurring theme in his work is his grinning face, which he does to spite techno producers who keep their identity hidden.
  10. Near the end of the song "Formula" on his Windowlicker single, when ran through a spectrogram analysis, James's face can be seen embedded as a steganogram.
